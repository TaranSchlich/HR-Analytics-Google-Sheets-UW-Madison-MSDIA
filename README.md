# HR Analytics in Google Sheets 
**By: Taran Schlichtmann**

**Date: 10/28/25**

Employee Turnover, Salary Analysis, and Workforce Insights

Focused on analyzing employee data using Google Sheets. The assignment simulates an HR analytics scenario where the goal is to understand salary expenses, turnover patterns, workforce demographics, and performance trends. All calculations, pivot tables, and visualizations were created directly in Google Sheets.

------------------------------------------------------------
Overview
------------------------------------------------------------

This assignment demonstrates proficiency in:

- Spreadsheet‑based data analysis  
- Pivot tables and filtering  
- Histogram, doughnut chart, column chart, and scatter plot creation  
- Workforce analytics (turnover, salary distribution, gender distribution)  
- Interpreting HR metrics to support business decisions  

The dataset includes employee demographics, compensation, employment status, tenure, and termination reasons.

Assignment Summary
------------------------------------------------------------

### Question 1 — Annual Salary Expense  
Calculated the total annual salary expense and created a histogram to visualize salary distribution.

**Result Insight:**  
Total annual salary expense: **19,428,635.20**

------------------------------------------------------------

### Question 2 — Gender Distribution of Active Employees  
Built a pivot table filtered to active employees and created a doughnut chart to visualize gender proportions.

**Result Insight:**  
Female employees represent **55.2%** of the active workforce.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Question 3 — Reasons for Voluntary Termination  
Created a pivot table filtered to voluntarily terminated employees and visualized results with a column chart.

**Result Insight:**  
**11** employees voluntarily left the company for **more money**.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Question 4 — Department Turnover  
Built a pivot table showing employment status by department and created a 100% stacked column chart to compare active vs. non‑active proportions.

**Result Insight:**  
The department with the **lowest proportion of active employees** was **Production**.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Question 5 — Relationship Between Age and Salary  
Created a scatter plot with a trendline to evaluate correlation between employee age and annual salary.

**Result Insight:**  
The R² value was **0.001**, indicating **no meaningful correlation** between age and salary.
